Tatzelwurm

Mythical Dragons

The Tatzelwurm, also known as the Stollenwurm or Stollwurm, is a mythical creature from Alpine folklore. This fascinating dragon-like being is said to inhabit the remote and rugged landscapes of the Alps, particularly in Austria, Switzerland, and Germany.

Described as having a serpentine body, feline or reptilian head, and sometimes short limbs, this mythical dragon has captivated imaginations for centuries. Historical accounts and sightings, such as those documented by Swiss naturalist Johann Jakob Scheuchzer, have kept the legend alive.

One charming tribute to this creature is the Tatzelwurm Fountain in Kobern-Gondorf, Germany. The Stollenwurm continues to inspire popular culture and remains a symbol of mystery and adventure in the Alpine wilderness.
